# Race Profile Questions (Generalized)

Use these 10 questions to build a world-agnostic race profile. Avoid named places, kingdoms, cities, or setting-specific proper nouns.

1. What origin narratives are associated with this race, and where do accounts differ or remain uncertain?
2. What core survival pressures historically shaped this race's behavior and values?
3. Which social structures are most common (clans, houses, councils, bands, guilds, loose networks)?
4. What roles or labors are most culturally typical in day-to-day life (non-combat)?
5. Which inborn dispositions, capabilities, or physical tendencies of this race create friction or advantage in broader society?
6. What rites of passage or adulthood markers are culturally important?
7. How does this race usually approach conflict, including when diplomacy is preferred and when violence is considered legitimate?
8. What family and kinship norms are typical (child-rearing, elders, obligation, inheritance)?
9. What are common misunderstandings outsiders have about this race, and what is usually true instead?
10. What generalized character hooks fit this race for play (duty, ambition, exile, craft, faith, curiosity, survival)?
